## What is this?

`@vita-tak/ai-logger` is an SDK that lets you add automatic AI compliance logging to your application in minutes. Every AI call gets logged and cryptographically stamped on the Minima blockchain — giving you a tamper-proof audit trail for EU AI Act compliance.

`autoLog()` wraps your existing AI client like a transparent layer. Your code keeps working exactly as before — logging happens silently in the background.

## How autoLog() works

autoLog() is framework-agnostic. It does not care whether your application is built with Express, Next.js, a CLI tool, or a plain script. The only thing that matters is three steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a provider client">
    Instantiate your OpenAI or Anthropic client as normal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wrap it with autoLog()">
    Pass the client to `aiLogger.autoLog(client)` — this is the only change needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the wrapped variable for all AI calls">
    Use `client` instead of your original instance. Every call through it is automatically logged and blockchain-stamped.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Think of autoLog() as a checkpoint at a door — not the building itself. It does not matter what the surrounding code looks like. As long as every AI call passes through the wrapped client, it will be captured and logged.
</Info>

## Supported providers at the moment

| Provider  | Method           | Status         |
|-----------|------------------|----------------|
| OpenAI    | autoLog(client)  | Auto-detected  |
| Anthropic | autoLog(client)  | Auto-detected  |

## Works with AI agents too

autoLog() works equally well with AI agents and multi-step orchestration systems — as long as the underlying OpenAI or Anthropic client is wrapped with autoLog(). Every call the agent makes, regardless of which tool or step triggers it, will be captured and logged automatically.

<Note>
  This applies to custom-built agents and simple orchestration logic. Frameworks that manage their own internal AI client instances (such as LangChain) require a separate integration.
</Note>
